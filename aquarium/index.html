What Is Clownfish?

Clownfish are social fish, communicating through popping and clicking noises. They live in groups of male fish with one dominant female, a dominant male, and a group of smaller males. The dominant male ensures its position by taking the best food opportunities.

Why are they called clownfish?
Clownfish are active and territorial fish with an erratic swimming pattern, who sometimes appear to be doing acrobatics while defending the area around their anemone. This behavior, combined with their bright coloration, led people to start calling them clownfish.
